---

**Problem:**

Consider a regular tetrahedron \(ABCD\) with edge length 2. Let \(M\) be the midpoint of edge \(CD\). A sequence of moves is defined where each move consists of moving from one vertex to an adjacent vertex. Starting at vertex \(A\), how many sequences of 10 moves are there such that the path does not pass through vertex \(M\) at any step?

---

**Solution:**

To solve this problem, we need to understand the movement structure within the tetrahedron and account for the restriction that the path cannot pass through \(M\).

1. **Vertices and Adjacency:**
   - The tetrahedron \(ABCD\) has vertices \(A, B, C,\) and \(D\).
   - Each vertex is adjacent to the other three vertices.

2. **Midpoint \(M\):**
   - \(M\) is the midpoint of \(CD\), and it is not a vertex of the tetrahedron, so it does not affect adjacency directly but influences the problem as a constraint.

3. **Movement and Restriction:**
   - We start at \(A\) and need to make 10 moves to another vertex.
   - The path must not pass through \(M\), which means we must avoid the edge \(CD\) at any step.

4. **State Representation:**
   - Let \(a_n\), \(b_n\), and \(c_n\) represent the number of sequences of \(n\) moves ending at vertices \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) (or \(D\)) respectively, where the path does not pass through \(M\).

5. **Recurrence Relations:**
   - From \(A\), we can move to \(B\), \(C\), or \(D\).
   - From \(B\), we can move to \(A\), \(C\), or \(D\).
   - From \(C\) (or \(D\)), we can move to \(A\), \(B\), or the other vertex.

6. **Initial Conditions:**
   - \(a_0 = 1\), \(b_0 = 0\), \(c_0 = 0\) (since we start at \(A\)).

7. **Recurrence Equations:**
   \[
   a_{n+1} = b_n + c_n + d_n
   \]
   \[
   b_{n+1} = a_n + c_n + d_n
   \]
   \[
   c_{n+1} = a_n + b_n + d_n
   \]
   where \(d_n\) is analogous to \(c_n\) since \(C\) and \(D\) are symmetrical.

8. **Simplification:**
   - Since \(b_n = c_n = d_n\) due to symmetry, let \(b_n = c_n = d_n = x_n\).
   - Then the recurrence relations become:
   \[
   a_{n+1} = 3x_n
   \]
   \[
   x_{n+1} = a_n + 2x_n
   \]

9. **Solve the Recurrence:**
   - Substitute \(a_n = 3x_{n-1}\) into \(x_{n+1} = a_n + 2x_n\):
   \[
   x_{n+1} = 3x_{n-1} + 2x_n
   \]

10. **Initial Terms:**
    - \(a_0 = 1\), \(x_0 = 0\)
    - \(a_1 = 3\), \(x_1 = 1\)

11. **Compute Terms up to \(n = 10\):**
    - \(a_2 = 3x_1 = 3 \cdot 1 = 3\)
    - \(x_2 = 3x_0 + 2x_1 = 0 + 2 \cdot 1 = 2\)
    - \(a_3 = 3x_2 = 3 \cdot 2 = 6\)
    - \(x_3 = 3x_1 + 2x_2 = 3 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot 2 = 7\)
    - \(a_4 = 3x_3 = 3 \cdot 7 = 21\)
    - \(x_4 = 3x_2 + 2x_3 = 3 \cdot 2 + 2 \cdot 7 = 20\)
    - \(a_5 = 3x_4 = 3 \cdot 20 = 60\)
    - \(x_5 = 3x_3 + 2x_4 = 3 \cdot 7 + 2 \cdot 20 = 71\)
    - \(a_6 = 3x_5 = 3 \cdot 71 = 213\)
    - \(x_6 = 3x_4 + 2x_5 = 3 \cdot 20 + 2 \cdot 71 = 202\)
    - \(a_7 = 3x_6 = 3 \cdot 202 = 606\)
    - \(x_7 = 3x_5 + 2x_6 = 3 \cdot 71 + 2 \cdot 202 = 617\)
    - \(a_8 = 3x_7 = 3 \cdot 617 = 1851\)
    - \(x_8 = 3x_6 + 2x_7 = 3 \cdot 202 + 2 \cdot 617 = 1840\)
    - \(a_9 = 3x_8 = 3 \cdot 1840 = 5520\)
    - \(x_9 = 3x_7 + 2x_8 = 3 \cdot 617 + 2 \cdot 1840 = 5713\)
    - \(a_{10} = 3x_9 = 3 \cdot 5713 = 17139\)

The total number of sequences of 10 moves starting at \(A\) and not passing through \(M\) is \(a_{10} + 3x_{10}\).

12. **Final Calculation:**
    - \(x_{10} = 3x_8 + 2x_9 = 3 \cdot 1840 + 2 \cdot 5713 = 17161\)
    - Total sequences = \(a_{10} + 3x_{10} = 17139 + 3 \cdot 17161 = 17139 + 51483 = 68622\).

Thus, the number of sequences of 10 moves starting at \(A\) and not passing through \(M\) is \(\boxed{68622}\).
